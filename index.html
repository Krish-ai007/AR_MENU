Setup Guide
1

Film on pure matte black

Use matte black cloth only. Light from above and both sides. The max-channel shader only removes true black — dark food (brown patty, red sauce, dark herbs) is never erased.

2

ML surface detection (MiDaS)

On camera start, the MiDaS depth model loads once (~15 MB, browser-cached). It scans the bottom 40% of the camera frame 4× per second, finds the flattest horizontal region (the table), and anchors the dish there — no manual tilt needed.

3

QR codes carry only IDs

?rid=…&iid=… only. No video URL exposed. Firebase fetches the dish on the customer's device.

4

Drag to rotate · Pinch to resize

Once the dish appears, drag left/right for 360° spin. Pinch to scale. Add ?debug=1 to the URL to see the live depth heatmap overlay during testing.

🧠 How MiDaS Works Here

MiDaS (Mixed-Data Supervised) is a monocular depth model — it estimates distance for every pixel from a single camera image. The integration runs it on a 320×240 downscaled frame, scans rows in the bottom 40% for the one with the lowest depth variance (flattest = table surface), smoothly lerps the dish Y position toward that row, and locks after 3 consecutive confident readings.

⚡ Shooting Tips

Leave 5–8 cm between dish and cloth edge. Use two lights at 45° to avoid shadows. Slightly overexpose the dish so it's always brighter than the background.
